What actually happens to your English under pressure.

When multiple demands hit your working memory at the same time in a high stakes English conversation processing fast speech, managing social perception, formulating grammar, and monitoring your own output capacity gets exceeded. In that overload moment, second language vocabulary is often the first casualty.

You are also aware that you are underperforming, and that awareness creates another meta cognitive monitoring layer. That extra layer steals even more cognitive bandwidth, performance drops again, and shame rises with every sentence.

This is not a knowledge problem. Your English is good enough. It is a deployment problem. The language knowledge exists, but under stress you cannot access it reliably enough to perform.

Your English is good enough. You just cannot access it yet when it counts.
